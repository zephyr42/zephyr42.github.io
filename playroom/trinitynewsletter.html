Trinity Washington Newsletter

For LSC652 Foundations of Digital Libraries, I collaborated with three other students to present a small collection of the Trinity Washington University's Trinity College Newsletter from the late 1950's to the early 1960's. This newsletter showcased the accomplishments of alumni, faculty, and students of the time as well as chronicled a period of growth and construction of the university's grounds. In the 1950's and '60s, Trinity was known for educating women scientists and politicians, many of whom went on to influence the world from here in the United States and abroad in Europe. Trinity now carries its tradition of educating women who may be overlooked by other institutions and providing them with the tools to learn and grow.

The twenty issues we scanned and described using DCMI can be found on the class CONTENTdm Server.
